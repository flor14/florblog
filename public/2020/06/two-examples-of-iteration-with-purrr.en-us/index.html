Two examples of iteration with purrr - Class for the R-Studio certification

21 June 2020 in Teaching

Certification

I recently became certified as a R-Studio Tidyverse instructor. To pass the certification there are two exams, one specific a subject (such as Tidyverse or Shiny) and another one for teaching. In this blog I am going to share a 15 minute class that I prepared to pass the teaching exam.

What to teach about?

I use loops frecquently in for my work. I decided to look for two specific examples of functions in purrr package that could be motivating for the students.

My two examples:

  • Read a group of files with the same extension and get a dataframe using map_df()

  • Generate Rmarkdown reports from a list of parameters automatically with pwalk()

Important

  • These 15 minutes are meant to be explained AFTER loops and purrr package and Rmarkdown. In particular, to answer the parts of “Let’s Practice” you need concepts that were explained before.

  • It is not necessary to use Xaringan for the certification slides. I moved the original slides to Xaringan for this post and made a few changes. It is important that you practice your class keeping track of how long it lasts.
